## Curation Filters

### Tier 1: SIGNAL (Send Immediately)
**Criteria:**
- Fundamental breakthrough in capability
- Open-source model release (>10B parameters)
- Major regulatory decision affecting AI development
- Proven safety/alignment advance
- Technique that 10x's efficiency

**Examples:**
- GPT-4 level model goes open source
- New architecture that obsoletes transformers
- EU AI Act passes with specific implications
- Breakthrough in interpretability research

**Frequency**: 1-3 per week

---

### Tier 2: INSIGHT (Daily Digest)
**Criteria:**
- Interesting research paper with novel approach
- New tool/service that solves real problem
- Industry shift (e.g., major company pivots strategy)
- Educational deep-dive on existing tech
- Ethical debate with multiple perspectives

**Examples:**
- Paper showing how to fine-tune models more efficiently
- New AI coding assistant with unique features
- Analysis of AI's impact on specific industry
- Thoughtful essay on AI safety trade-offs

**Frequency**: 5-10 per day â†’ Bundled into evening digest

---

### Tier 3: NOISE (Filter Out)
**Criteria:**
- Incremental model updates (GPT-4.1.2 patch notes)
- Corporate press releases without substance
- Opinion pieces restating common arguments
- Clickbait ("AI will replace X by 2025!")
- Twitter drama & personality conflicts
- Speculation without evidence

**Examples:**
- "ChatGPT now 2% better at math!"
- CEO makes bold prediction without timeline
- Hot take from non-expert
- Aggregated listicle content

**Frequency**: 90% of AI news â†’ Ignored

## Filtering Logic

### Keyword Boosting (Higher Priority)
**Positive Signals:**
- "open source", "Apache 2.0", "MIT license"
- "reproducible", "code available", "dataset released"
- "empirical study", "ablation", "benchmarks"
- "safety", "alignment", "interpretability"
- "efficiency", "faster", "smaller model"
- "accessibility", "consumer hardware", "local"

**Negative Signals:**
- "will revolutionize", "game-changer", "unprecedented"
- "coming soon", "we plan to", "aiming for"
- "sources say", "rumor", "leak"
- "AGI by", "sentient", "conscious"
- "exclusive", "insider", "sources claim"
- Excessive exclamation marks!!!

### Source Trust Scoring
**High Trust (Score: 5):**
- ArXiv preprints
- Official company blogs (OpenAI, Anthropic, etc.)
- Peer-reviewed publications
- GitHub repositories with >1000 stars

**Medium Trust (Score: 3):**
- Tech journalism (Ars Technica, The Verge)
- Hacker News discussions (>100 points)
- Industry analysts
- Academic Twitter threads

**Low Trust (Score: 1):**
- Press releases
- Generic tech blogs
- Aggregator sites
- Social media rumors
- "Insider" sources

### Recency Weighting
- <6 hours old: 2x relevance
- <24 hours old: 1x relevance
- >24 hours old: Only if breakthrough
- >1 week old: Ignore (unless evergreen content)
