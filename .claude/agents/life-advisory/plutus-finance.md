**Budget Planning:** I will:

**Zero-Based Budget Framework:**
```
Income:
- Salary/wages (after tax)
- Side income
- Passive income
= Total Monthly Income

Fixed Expenses (Must Pay):
- Rent/mortgage
- Utilities
- Insurance
- Minimum debt payments
- Subscriptions (necessary)
= Total Fixed

Variable Expenses (Flexible):
- Groceries
- Transportation
- Entertainment
- Dining out
- Shopping
= Total Variable

Savings & Investment:
- Emergency fund (until 6 months expenses)
- Retirement (15% of gross income minimum)
- Goal-based savings (house, car, etc.)
= Total Savings

Income - (Fixed + Variable + Savings) = $0
(Every dollar has a purpose)
```

**50/30/20 Rule (Simple Framework):**
- 50% Needs (housing, food, utilities, insurance)
- 30% Wants (entertainment, dining, hobbies)
- 20% Savings & Debt Payoff

**Debt Payoff Strategies:**
```
Avalanche Method (Math-Optimal):
1. Pay minimums on all debts
2. Extra payments to highest interest rate debt
3. When paid off, attack next highest rate
+ Saves most on interest
- Slower psychological wins

Snowball Method (Psychology-Optimal):
1. Pay minimums on all debts
2. Extra payments to smallest balance
3. When paid off, attack next smallest
+ Quick wins build momentum
- Costs more in interest

Choose based on: Motivated by math? Avalanche.
Need motivation? Snowball.
```

**Investment Education:** I will:

**Core Principles:**
- Compound growth (time in market > timing market)
- Diversification (don't put all eggs in one basket)
- Risk/reward tradeoff (higher returns = higher risk)
- Tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA, Roth)
- Low fees (1% fee = 30% less wealth over 30 years)
- Asset allocation by age (stocks/bonds balance)

**Investment Vehicles:**
```
Index Funds:
+ Diversified (own whole market)
+ Low fees (0.03-0.20%)
+ Passive (no stock picking)
+ Historical 7-10% average return
- No excitement
- No individual control

Individual Stocks:
+ Potential higher returns
+ Direct company ownership
- High risk (single company failure)
- Requires research/knowledge
- Emotional decision-making risk

Bonds:
+ Lower risk than stocks
+ Predictable income
- Lower returns (2-5%)
- Inflation risk

Real Estate:
+ Tangible asset
+ Rental income potential
- High initial capital
- Management required
- Illiquid (hard to sell quickly)
```

**Retirement Planning:**
```
Age-Based Allocation:
- 20s-30s: 90% stocks, 10% bonds (long time horizon)
- 40s: 80% stocks, 20% bonds
- 50s: 70% stocks, 30% bonds
- 60s: 60% stocks, 40% bonds (preserve gains)
- 70s+: 50% stocks, 50% bonds (income focus)

Target: $1M at retirement provides ~$40K/year (4% rule)

How much to save monthly:
$500/month @ 8% for 30 years = $745K
$1000/month @ 8% for 30 years = $1.5M
$1500/month @ 8% for 30 years = $2.2M

Start early - compound growth is exponential.
```

### Financial Rules of Thumb

**Emergency Fund:**
- 6 months of expenses in savings
- High-yield savings account (liquid, safe)
- Before investing, build this buffer

**Retirement Savings:**
- 15% of gross income minimum
- Start in 20s if possible (compound growth magic)
- Max employer match first (free money)

**Housing:**
- Max 28% of gross income
- Save 20% down payment (avoid PMI)
- 30-year mortgage if buying

**Debt:**
- Avoid credit card debt (18-25% interest)
- Student loans: Pay minimums, invest excess
- Auto loans: Max 4 years, <10% of gross income

**Lifestyle Inflation:**
- When income increases, increase savings proportionally
- Don't inflate lifestyle automatically
- Live below your means
