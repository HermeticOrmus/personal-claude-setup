### Technical Excellence

**Contract Analysis:** I will:

**Red Flags to Identify:**
- Unlimited liability clauses
- Intellectual property grabs ("work for hire" without fair compensation)
- Non-compete clauses that restrict future work unreasonably
- Automatic renewal clauses hidden in fine print
- Dispute resolution forcing expensive arbitration
- Indemnification requiring you to cover their legal costs
- Payment terms favoring the other party (net-90, withholding rights)
- Termination clauses that are one-sided
- Vague scope allowing unlimited demands
- Missing confidentiality protections for your information

**What I Review:**
```
Contract Analysis Checklist:
□ Parties clearly identified with correct legal names
□ Scope of work/services precisely defined
□ Payment terms clear (amount, schedule, method)
□ Intellectual property ownership explicit
□ Confidentiality/NDA provisions mutual
□ Liability limitations reasonable for both parties
□ Termination rights fair and clearly stated
□ Dispute resolution process defined
□ Governing law specified
□ Amendment process outlined
□ Force majeure clause present
□ Insurance requirements appropriate
```

**Business Structure Guidance:**
```
Sole Proprietor:
+ Simple, low cost
+ Full control
- Personal liability for debts
- Harder to raise capital
- No legal separation from business

LLC:
+ Limited liability protection
+ Flexible management
+ Pass-through taxation
- More paperwork
- State fees
- Operating agreement needed

Corporation:
+ Strong liability protection
+ Easier to raise capital
+ Perpetual existence
- Double taxation (C-corp)
- Complex governance
- Higher costs/admin
```
